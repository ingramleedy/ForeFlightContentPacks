# Special Airspace and Documented Overflight Requests — Tribal Lands

This document covers the specific, citable cases where tribal lands intersect published airspace rules or documented voluntary overflight requests. It is not exhaustive. Always check current sectional charts, Chart Supplement, and NOTAMs.

## 1. Grand Canyon Special Flight Rules Area — 14 CFR 93 Subpart U

The Grand Canyon SFRA is a **federally enforceable** rule covering all aircraft operations within the defined lateral boundaries. It overlaps:

- **Hualapai Indian Reservation** (western Grand Canyon, south rim)
- **Havasupai Reservation** (inside Grand Canyon)
- **Navajo Nation** (eastern Grand Canyon, north/east portions)

Key elements:

- **Flight-Free Zones** — aircraft are prohibited in the Desert View, Bright Angel, Shinumo, and Toroweap/Thunder River flight-free zones below specified altitudes.
- **Flight Corridors** — Dragon, Zuni Point, and Fossil Canyon corridors allow transits with altitude restrictions, typically 10,500 ft MSL or higher depending on direction and corridor.
- **Air Tour Operators** — additional requirements under 14 CFR 93.309–93.325 and NPATMA.

**Action:** review the current FAA Grand Canyon Special Flight Rules Area chart and 14 CFR 93 Subpart U before any flight in the Grand Canyon region.

Source: https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14/chapter-I/subchapter-F/part-93/subpart-U

## 2. Taos Pueblo (New Mexico) — sectional-noted voluntary avoidance

The Albuquerque sectional carries a published advisory asking pilots to avoid flight below specific altitudes over Taos Pueblo lands, including the Taos Pueblo World Heritage Site and Blue Lake area. A proposed operator agreement has referenced a voluntary **5,000 ft AGL** ceiling.

The note is advisory, not regulatory, but it appears on the sectional because Taos Pueblo is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the request has been formally coordinated with the FAA.

**Action:** stay at or above 2,000 ft AGL at minimum; 5,000 ft AGL preferred when routing near Taos Pueblo.

Source: current Albuquerque Sectional Chart and New Mexico Chart Supplement (Taos area A/FD entry). https://www.faa.gov/air_traffic/flight_info/aeronav/

## 3. Air Tour Management Plans (ATMPs) under NPATMA

NPATMA requires ATMPs or voluntary agreements for commercial air tour operations over National Parks and tribal lands within or abutting them. Parks with ATMPs or voluntary agreements that affect tribal-adjacent airspace include (non-exhaustive):

- Glen Canyon NRA (Navajo)
- Canyon de Chelly NM (Navajo — the park itself is on Navajo Nation land)
- Mesa Verde NP (Ute Mountain)
- Badlands NP (Pine Ridge area)
- Rainbow Bridge NM (Navajo)

Part 91 recreational pilots are generally not subject to ATMPs, but **commercial tour operators are**. See the current plans at https://www.nps.gov/subjects/sound/airtourmanagement.htm

## 4. Red Lake Reservation (Minnesota) — local tribal resolution, not FAA-codified

The Red Lake Band of Chippewa has passed tribal resolutions asserting restrictions on overflights below 20,000 ft MSL. **These are not codified in FAA regulation** and do not appear on sectional charts. The FAA retains exclusive authority over airspace, so compliance is not legally required at the federal level.

However, tribal law enforcement has, in isolated incidents, pursued ground-based consequences against pilots following landing or land activities on the reservation. This is included here for **situational awareness**, not as an airspace restriction.

**Action:** compliance with AC 91-36D (2,000 ft AGL over noise-sensitive areas) is the FAA's position. If you intend to land or operate from Red Lake, coordinate with tribal authorities in advance.

## 5. General noise-sensitive tribal lands

Many other reservations appear under the AC 91-36D guidance without specific chart notes — Hopi, Zuni, Acoma, Jicarilla Apache, Mescalero Apache, Wind River, Flathead, Standing Rock, Pine Ridge, and others have requested the 2,000 ft AGL courtesy at various times.

There is no single public database of these requests. The reliable sources remain:

- Current sectional chart notes
- Chart Supplement (Airport/Facility Directory) entries for reservation airports
- Active NOTAMs
- Direct coordination with tribal aviation liaisons

## 6. How to research specific reservations before a flight

1. Open the sectional for the area — read any printed advisory notes over the reservation polygon.
2. Check the Chart Supplement entries for airports on or near the reservation — tribal-specific guidance often appears in airport remarks.
3. Pull NOTAMs for the area via 1800wxbrief.com or your EFB.
4. For commercial or repeated operations, contact the FAA regional office. Tribal aviation liaisons exist under FAA Order 1210.20.
5. BIA Tribal Leaders Directory provides official tribal government contact information: https://www.bia.gov/service/tribal-leaders-directory
