# Red Lake Reservation

## About

The Red Lake Indian Reservation is the home of the Red Lake Band of Chippewa Indians (Red Lake Nation) in north-central Minnesota. The reservation covers approximately 1,260 square miles across eight counties, including most of Upper and Lower Red Lake. It is one of only two "closed" reservations in the United States, where tribal land is held in common by the tribe rather than allocated to individual members under the Dawes Act. Tribal enrollment is approximately 12,000.

## Aviation notes — READ CAREFULLY

- **Red Lake Band Resolution No. 59-78 (1978)** prohibits aircraft operations below 20,000 ft over the reservation. This tribal resolution is **not** on FAA sectional charts and is **not** enforceable against pilots under federal law. The FAA retains exclusive authority over navigable airspace.
- **The tribal assertion matters once wheels are on the ground.** The Red Lake Nation has demonstrated that it will exercise tribal jurisdiction over aircraft that land on the reservation, citing the 1978 resolution.
- **Documented incident (Smedsmo, 15 October 2025):** Pilot Darrin Smedsmo made a safe engine-out emergency landing in a 1946 Stinson 108 on a road within the reservation after engine failure at 3,500 ft over Lower Red Lake. Red Lake Nation impounded the aircraft. As of March 2026 the aircraft remains impounded; AOPA has requested federal intervention; the tribe offered a settlement of $5,000 to the Red Lake Boys & Girls Club plus a $2,750 towing fee, which Smedsmo rejected. Legal dispute ongoing.

## Operational guidance

- AC 91-36D advisory applies — aim for 2,000 ft AGL or higher over the reservation as a courtesy.
- **If you have any choice under 14 CFR 91.3/91.119 safety discretion, an emergency landing outside the reservation boundary is preferable** given the documented recovery complications.
- If you must land on the reservation:
  1. Safety first — occupants, egress, shutdown.
  2. Call 911. Contact the nearest FAA FSDO as soon as practical.
  3. **Contact AOPA Legal Services Plan BEFORE making statements to tribal authorities**: https://www.aopa.org/legal-services/emergency-legal-contacts
  4. Document everything — photos, timestamps, witness contacts, all interactions.
  5. Aircraft recovery will require tribal government coordination and may involve tribal court proceedings.
