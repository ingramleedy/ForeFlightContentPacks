# Hualapai Indian Reservation

## About

The Hualapai ("people of the tall pines") Indian Reservation lies along 108 miles of the Colorado River in northwestern Arizona, adjoining the southern rim of the Grand Canyon. The Hualapai Tribe operates Grand Canyon West, the Skywalk, and the Hualapai River Runners rafting operation on the Colorado River through the western Grand Canyon. Hualapai Airport (3AZ5) lies on the reservation.

## Aviation notes

- **Grand Canyon SFRA (14 CFR 93 Subpart U)** covers portions of the reservation adjoining the Grand Canyon. Flight-free zones, corridors, and altitude restrictions are federal regulation and enforceable.
- **Hualapai Tribe asserted authority over airspace up to 15,000 ft AGL** after a 2009 paraglider landing incident. The dispute settled before codified case law. The tribal position is not enforceable against airspace users but is relevant for any aircraft that lands on reservation ground.
- Commercial air tour activity over Hualapai lands is regulated by NPATMA and the Grand Canyon SFRA.
