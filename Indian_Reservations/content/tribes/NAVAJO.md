# Navajo Nation Reservation

## About

The Navajo Nation is the largest federally recognized tribe in the United States by enrolled members (~400,000) and the largest by land area (approximately 27,000 square miles across Arizona, New Mexico, and Utah). The Navajo Nation was established by the Treaty of Bosque Redondo in 1868. The Diné government operates with three branches (Legislative, Executive, Judicial) and a President elected to four-year terms.

## Aviation notes

- **Grand Canyon SFRA (14 CFR 93 Subpart U)** applies to portions of Navajo lands abutting the Grand Canyon on the north and east rims.
- **No general numeric overflight altitude is codified** in the Navajo Nation Code, but Navajo Nation Parks & Recreation prohibits aircraft and UAV operations over specified tribal parks without an approved flight plan:
  - Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park
  - Lake Powell Navajo Tribal Park
  - Tseyi Dine Heritage Area (Canyon de Chelly)
  - Little Colorado River Gorge Navajo Tribal Park
- Several public airports are on the reservation, including Chinle (E91), Kayenta (0V7), Window Rock (KRQE), and Shiprock (5V5).
- NPATMA Air Tour Management Plans affect commercial air tour operations over Canyon de Chelly and other Navajo-abutting parks.

## Nearby services

Reservation airports offer fuel service of varying availability. Chinle, Kayenta, and Window Rock are the most common transient stops. Check NOTAMs and call ahead.
