# Taos Pueblo

## About

Taos Pueblo is a multi-story adobe complex in northern New Mexico that has been continuously inhabited for over 1,000 years. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site (1992) and a U.S. National Historic Landmark. The pueblo is at 7,200 ft elevation in the foothills of the Sangre de Cristo range, about 1 mile north of Taos, New Mexico. Blue Lake (Ba Whyea), returned to the Pueblo by the federal government in 1970, is a sacred site in the nearby wilderness area.

## Aviation notes

- **Voluntary FAA / Taos Pueblo Memorandum of Agreement (December 2011)** asks pilots to remain **above 5,000 ft AGL** over the Pueblo, the World Heritage Site, and the Blue Lake Wilderness Area. This request is printed on the Albuquerque sectional and referenced in the New Mexico Chart Supplement.
- The Taos Pueblo Tribal Council has asserted the position since 1990. The MOA formalized the voluntary agreement through FAA tribal-consultation process.
- This is an **advisory**, not a federal regulation. It reflects a long-standing and well-documented tribal request that the FAA has chosen to help publicize via the sectional chart.
- Taos Regional Airport (KSKX) is approximately 7 NM southwest of the Pueblo and is the practical GA stop for the area.

## Operational guidance

If routing near Taos Pueblo:
- Maintain 5,000 ft AGL or higher over the Pueblo, the Pueblo lands, and the Blue Lake Wilderness when practical.
- If operating into or out of KSKX, comply with standard traffic-pattern altitudes; Taos Regional Airport's pattern does not overfly the Pueblo.
- Check NOTAMs — ceremonial events occasionally generate temporary flight restrictions over the Pueblo.
