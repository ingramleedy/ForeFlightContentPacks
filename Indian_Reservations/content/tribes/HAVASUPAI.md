# Havasupai Reservation

## About

The Havasupai Reservation lies at the bottom of the Grand Canyon in Havasu Canyon, a tributary of the Colorado River. The village of Supai is one of the most remote populated places in the contiguous United States — accessible only by an 8-mile hiking trail, by helicopter, or by mule. The Havasupai Tribe has inhabited the canyon for at least 800 years.

## Aviation notes

- **Grand Canyon SFRA (14 CFR 93 Subpart U)** covers the Havasupai Reservation. Flight-free zones, corridors, and altitude restrictions are federal regulation and enforceable.
- Havasupai Tribe operates a helicopter service (Airwest) between Hualapai Hilltop and Supai village. General-aviation access to Supai is not available.
- **No tribal overflight ordinance** asserting minimum altitudes has been located in public sources, beyond the Grand Canyon SFRA's federal rules.
- Landing in the Havasupai canyon outside the approved helicopter pad is not feasible and would put the aircraft in one of the most remote recovery situations in the U.S.
